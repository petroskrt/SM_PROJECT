Snake Plant

Your Guide to growing and caring for Snake Plants

About Snake Plants

Snake plants, also known as the snake plant, Saint George's sword, mother-in-law's tongue, and viper's bowstring hemp, among other names.This plant is often kept as a houseplant due to its non-demanding maintenance; they can survive with very little water and sun.

Care Requirements

Light

Tolerates low light but thrives in indirect bright light. Can handle some direct sun.

Water

Let soil dry between waterings. Water less in winter. Susceptible to root rot if overwatered.

Soil & Fertilizer

Well-draining potting mix. Fertilize lightly during growing season (spring/summer).

Benefits

  • Excellent air purifier, removing toxins like formaldehyde
  • Very low maintenance and drought tolerant
  • Can survive in various light conditions
  • Long-lasting and slow-growing
  • Perfect for bedroom as it releases oxygen at night

Common Problems

  • Yellow or brown leaves - Usually indicates overwatering
  • Drooping leaves - Could mean too much water or too little light
  • Brown tips - Often due to underwatering or low humidity
  • Root rot - Caused by prolonged wet soil conditions
