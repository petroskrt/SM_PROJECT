Jade Plant

Your Guide to growing and caring for Jade Plants

About Jade Plants

Jade plants, also known as money plants or lucky plants, are succulent houseplants native to South Africa. Known for their thick, woody stems and oval-shaped leaves, they can grow into small trees over time. In many cultures, they're considered symbols of prosperity and good fortune.

Care Requirements

Light

Bright, indirect light with some direct morning sun. At least 4-6 hours of bright light daily. South or west-facing windows are ideal. Protect from intense afternoon sun. Leaves may develop red tinges in bright light - this is normal and healthy.

Water

Allow soil to dry completely between waterings. Water thoroughly but infrequently. Reduce watering in winter. Signs of thirst include slightly wrinkled leaves. Better to underwater than overwater - jade plants are drought-tolerant succulents.

Temperature & Humidity

Ideal temperatures between 65-75°F (18-24°C). Can tolerate cooler temperatures but protect from frost. Prefer average to low humidity. Good air circulation is important.

Soil & Pot

Well-draining cactus or succulent mix. Add perlite or coarse sand for better drainage. Use pots with drainage holes. Terracotta pots are ideal as they help prevent overwatering by wicking away excess moisture.

Benefits

  • Very long-lived plants that can be passed down through generations
  • Believed to bring financial luck and prosperity in many cultures
  • Low maintenance and drought-tolerant
  • Can be pruned and shaped into beautiful bonsai-like forms
  • Excellent for beginners due to their resilience
  • Propagates easily from leaves or stem cuttings
  • Can flower in optimal conditions, producing small star-shaped white or pink blooms

Common Problems

  • Yellowing leaves - Usually indicates overwatering
  • Leaf drop - Can be caused by overwatering, underwatering, or cold drafts
  • Soft, mushy stems - Sign of severe overwatering or root rot
  • Wrinkled leaves - Plant needs water
  • Leggy growth - Insufficient light
  • Black spots on leaves - Could be edema from inconsistent watering
  • Root rot - Caused by poor drainage or overwatering

Propagation Guide

Jade plants can be propagated in two main ways:

Leaf Propagation

  • Gently twist off a healthy leaf
  • Let it callus for 2-3 days
  • Place on top of well-draining soil
  • Mist occasionally until roots develop

Stem Cuttings

  • Cut a healthy stem 3-4 inches long
  • Remove lower leaves
  • Allow cut end to dry for several days
  • Plant in slightly moist, well-draining soil

Pruning & Maintenance

  • Prune in spring or summer to maintain shape and encourage bushiness
  • Remove any dead, yellowed, or damaged leaves
  • Support heavy branches to prevent breaking
  • Clean leaves occasionally to remove dust
  • Repot every 2-3 years or when root-bound

Tips for Success

  • Rotate plant regularly to ensure even growth
  • Use a fast-draining pot slightly larger than the root ball
  • Watch for signs of pests like mealybugs, particularly in branch joints
  • Reduce water and stop fertilizing in winter months
  • Can be moved outdoors in summer but acclimatize gradually
