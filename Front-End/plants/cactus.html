Cactus

Your Guide to growing and caring for Desert Cacti

About Cacti

Cacti are succulent plants native to the Americas, specifically adapted to survive in arid conditions. They come in a vast array of shapes and sizes, from tiny ball cacti to towering saguaros. Their unique ability to store water and specialized growth pattern make them fascinating and relatively low-maintenance houseplants.

Care Requirements

Light

Bright, direct sunlight for 4-6 hours daily is ideal. South or west-facing windows are perfect. New plants should be gradually acclimated to prevent sunburn. Most species need strong light to maintain their shape and promote flowering.

Water

Water thoroughly but infrequently - allow soil to dry completely between waterings. General rule: water every 1-2 weeks during growing season (spring/summer) and monthly or less in winter. Always err on the side of underwatering rather than overwatering.

Soil & Drainage

Fast-draining cactus mix is essential. Mix regular potting soil with coarse sand, pumice, or perlite for better drainage. Always use pots with drainage holes. Consider top-dressing with gravel or small stones.

Benefits

  • Extremely drought-tolerant and low-maintenance
  • Perfect for busy people or frequent travelers
  • Many species produce beautiful flowers when well-cared for
  • Long-living plants that can survive for decades
  • Available in fascinating shapes and sizes for unique decoration
  • Excellent choice for sunny windowsills where other plants might struggle
  • Most species are very slow-growing, requiring minimal pruning or maintenance

Common Problems

  • Rotting base or roots - Almost always indicates overwatering
  • Wrinkled or shriveled appearance - Signs of underwatering
  • Pale, yellow, or bleached appearance - Too much direct sun without acclimation
  • Elongated, stretched growth - Insufficient light (etiolation)
  • White, cottony patches - Mealybugs infestation
  • Corking (brown, bark-like tissue at base) - Natural aging process, not harmful

Seasonal Care

Growing Season (Spring/Summer)

  • Rotate the plant regularly to ensure even growth
  • Remove any dead or yellowing fronds at the base to maintain appearance
  • Clean fronds occasionally with a damp cloth to remove dust
  • Repot every 2-3 years or when roots begin growing through drainage holes
  • Consider moving outdoors to a shaded location during warm months
  • Divide large plants in spring to create new specimens

Dormant Season (Fall/Winter)

  • Reduce watering significantly
  • Stop fertilizing
  • Maintain cooler temperatures
  • Continue providing bright light

Safety Note

Always handle cacti with care - use thick gloves or folded newspaper when repotting or handling. Some species have very fine glochids (tiny barbed spines) that can be difficult to remove from skin. Keep out of reach of children and pets.

Propagation Tips

Cacti can be propagated though:

  • Offsets or "pups" - carefully remove and allow cut to callus before planting
  • Stem cuttings - cut a segment, let it callus, then plant in dry soil
  • Seeds - requires patience and specific conditions for germination
