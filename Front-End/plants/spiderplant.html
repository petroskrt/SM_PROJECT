Spider Plant

Your Guide to growing and caring for Spider Plants

About Spider Plants

Chlorophytum comosum, usually called spider plant or common spider plant due to its spider-like look,is a species of evergreen perennial flowering plant of the family Asparagaceae. It is native to tropical and Southern Africa but has become naturalized in other parts of the world, including Western Australia and Bangladesh. It is easy to grow as a houseplant because of its resilience, but it can be sensitive to the fluoride in tap water, which commonly gives it "burnt tips".

Care Requirements

Light

The Spider plant grows best in indirect light. While it can tolerate some direct sun in the morning, too much full sunlight will burn its leaves. Filtered light is the best option for this green beauty. Your plant should receive only 4 to 6 hours of bright light daily to prevent leaf issues. The best location for your Spider plant would be an east or west-facing window. Furthermore, it can also adapt to low light, but won't be as lush.

Water

Water the spider plant about once a week. Letting the soil dry out a little between waterings is a good idea. If you start to see fungus growing, yellowed leaves, or root rot occurring on your spider plant, you may be overwatering.

Temperature & Humidity

Prefers 60-80° F (15.5-24.6°C). Your Spider Plant will do well in normal household humidity but will thrive with a bit more humidity. Brown leaf tips may indicate the air is too dry, so mist your Spider Plant regularly using a Mister.

Soil & Pot

well-draining potting mix. A mixture of houseplant soil, perlite, and vermiculite works well. You can also add a bit of sand for extra drainage.

Benefits

  • Excellent air purifier
  • Non-toxic to pets and children
  • Easy to propagate from "spiderettes"
  • Highly resilient and adaptable
  • Visually striking with arching foliage

Common Problems

  • Brown leaf tips - Often caused by fluoride or chlorine in water; use distilled or rainwater
  • Yellowing leaves - Overwatering or poor drainage
  • Spiderettes not forming - May need brighter light
  • Root rot - Caused by waterlogged soil
  • Pests like spider mites or aphids - Treat with insecticidal soap

Propagation Guide

Spiderette Method

  • Choose a healthy spiderette (baby plant)
  • Cut from parent plant or keep attached
  • Place in water or directly in moist soil
  • Roots should develop in 2-3 weeks
  • Once established, pot in fresh soil

Pruning & Maintenance

  • Trim brown tips to maintain appearance
  • Remove yellow or damaged leaves
  • Cut off spiderettes if not desired
  • Repot every 1-2 years as needed

Tips for Success

  • Avoid overwatering; ensure soil dries slightly between waterings
  • Flush soil occasionally to remove salt buildup
  • Use balanced fertilizer monthly during growing season
  • Rotate plant regularly for even growth
  • Group with other plants to increase humidity naturally
