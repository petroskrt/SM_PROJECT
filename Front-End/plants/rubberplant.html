Rubber Plant

Your Guide to growing and caring for Rubber Plants

About Rubber Plants

Rubber plants (Ficus elastica) are popular houseplants known for their glossy, leather-like leaves and upright growth habit. Native to Southeast Asia, they can grow into tall indoor trees and are valued for their air-purifying qualities.

Care Requirements

Light

Bright, indirect light. Can tolerate some direct morning sun. Protect from harsh afternoon sun. Variegated varieties need brighter light to maintain their coloring.

Water

Allow top 2 inches of soil to dry between waterings. Water less in winter. Use lukewarm water and avoid getting leaves wet. Empty drainage tray after watering.

Well-draining potting mix rich in organic matter. Mix in perlite for better drainage. Use pots with drainage holes. Size up pot by 2 inches when repotting.

Benefits

  • Excellent air purifier, removing indoor toxins
  • Low maintenance once established
  • Can grow into impressive indoor trees
  • Long-lasting, glossy foliage
  • Available in various leaf colors and patterns
  • Drought tolerant when mature
  • Resistant to most pests

Common Problems

  • Leaf drop - Usually from temperature shock or overwatering
  • Brown edges - Low humidity or inconsistent watering
  • Yellow leaves - Overwatering or poor drainage
  • Leggy growth - Insufficient light
  • Leaf spots - Could be sunburn or fungal infection
  • White sap dripping - Normal when leaves are damaged
  • Dusty leaves - Regular cleaning needed

Propagation Guide

Stem Cutting Method

  • Cut 6-inch stem with 2-3 leaves
  • Allow white sap to stop flowing
  • Dip in rooting hormone (optional)
  • Plant in moist potting mix
  • Maintain warm temperature and humidity
  • Roots develop in 3-4 weeks

Pruning & Maintenance

  • Wear gloves when pruning (sap can irritate skin)
  • Prune in spring to control height and shape
  • Clean leaves monthly with damp cloth
  • Remove yellow or damaged leaves
  • Repot every 2-3 years in spring

Tips for Success

  • Keep away from pets (toxic if ingested)
  • Rotate plant quarterly for even growth
  • Support larger plants with stakes
  • Fertilize monthly during growing season
  • Consider branch notching to encourage bushiness
