Peace Lily

Your Guide to growing and caring for Peace Lilies

About Peace Lilies

Peace lilies are elegant indoor plants known for their dark green leaves and distinctive white flowers. They're popular houseplants due to their adaptability and air-purifying qualities. Despite their name, they're not true lilies but members of the Araceae family.

Care Requirements

Light

Prefers medium to low indirect light. Avoid direct sunlight as it can burn the leaves. Can tolerate fluorescent lights, making them perfect for offices.

Water

Keep soil consistently moist but not waterlogged. Water when top inch of soil feels dry. They dramatically droop when thirsty but recover quickly after watering. Prefer distilled or filtered water as they're sensitive to fluoride and chlorine.

Humidity & Temperature

Enjoys high humidity (50% or higher). Mist regularly or use a humidity tray. Thrives in temperatures between 65-80°F (18-27°C). Keep away from cold drafts.

Soil & Fertilizer

Well-draining, rich potting mix. Fertilize every 6-8 weeks during growing season with balanced, water-soluble fertilizer at half strength.

Benefits

  • Excellent air purifier, removing common indoor pollutants like benzene, formaldehyde, and carbon monoxide
  • Acts as a natural humidifier, adding moisture to the air through transpiration
  • Clear indicator of watering needs through leaf drooping
  • Produces beautiful white flowers throughout the year
  • Tolerant of low light conditions, perfect for indoor spaces
  • Relatively low maintenance once optimal conditions are met

Common Problems

  • Yellow leaves - Usually indicates overwatering or poor drainage
  • Brown leaf tips - Often caused by tap water sensitivity, low humidity, or too much fertilizer
  • No flowers - Insufficient light or improper fertilization
  • Drooping leaves - Typically underwatering, but can also be from overwatering if accompanied by yellowing
  • Black-tipped roots - Root rot from overwatering
  • Leaf spots - Can be caused by bacterial or fungal infections, often due to overhead watering

Notes

The peace lily is mildly toxic to humans and other animals when ingested. Like many Araceae, it contains calcium oxalate crystals, which can cause skin irritation, a burning sensation in the mouth, difficulty swallowing, and nausea.
