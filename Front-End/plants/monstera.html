Monstera

Your Guide to growing and caring for Monstera Deliciosa

About Monstera

Monstera deliciosa, also known as the Swiss Cheese Plant or Split-Leaf Philodendron, is a tropical plant native to Central America. Famous for its large, perforated leaves and climbing nature, it's become one of the most popular houseplants worldwide.

Care Requirements

Light

Bright, indirect light. Avoid direct sunlight which can burn leaves. Can tolerate moderate shade but may develop smaller leaves with fewer fenestrations. East or north-facing windows are ideal.

Water

Water when top 2-3 inches of soil feels dry. Maintain consistent moisture but avoid waterlogging. Reduce watering in winter. Use room temperature water and ensure good drainage.

Benefits

  • Natural air purifier that removes indoor air pollutants
  • Creates a dramatic tropical aesthetic with its large leaves
  • Relatively easy to care for once established
  • Can grow quite large, making a stunning statement piece
  • Long-lived when properly cared for
  • Adaptable to various indoor environments
  • Leaves develop more splits and holes as the plant matures

Common Problems

  • Yellow leaves - Usually indicates overwatering or poor drainage
  • Brown leaf edges - Low humidity or inconsistent watering
  • No fenestrations - Insufficient light or immature plant
  • Leggy growth - Not enough light
  • Root rot - Overwatering or poor drainage
  • Leaf spots - Could be fungal infection from wet leaves
  • Drooping leaves - Usually underwatering or temperature stress

Propagation Guide

Stem Cutting Propagation

  • Cut below a node with at least one leaf and aerial root
  • Place in water or moist potting mix
  • Maintain warm temperature and high humidity
  • Wait 4-6 weeks for roots to develop
  • Transfer to soil once roots are 2-3 inches long

Pruning & Maintenance

  • Regularly dust leaves with a damp cloth
  • Remove yellow or damaged leaves at the base
  • Train climbing vines onto support structures
  • Prune aerial roots if desired (though best to leave them)
  • Repot every 2-3 years or when roots emerge from drainage holes

Tips for Success

  • Install a support pole early in the plant's life
  • Rotate regularly to ensure even growth
  • Mist leaves occasionally to increase humidity
  • Fertilize monthly during growing season with balanced fertilizer
  • Clean leaves monthly to maximize light absorption
