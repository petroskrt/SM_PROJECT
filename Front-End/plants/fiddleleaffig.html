Fiddle Leaf Fig

Your Guide to growing and caring for Fiddle Leaf Figs

About Fiddle Leaf Figs

Fiddle Leaf Figs are popular indoor trees known for their large, violin-shaped leaves and impressive height. Native to western Africa's tropical rainforests, they've become a favorite statement plant in modern interior design. While beautiful, they can be somewhat challenging to care for as they're sensitive to environmental changes.

Care Requirements

Light

Bright, indirect light is essential. Can tolerate some direct morning sun but protect from harsh afternoon rays. Rotate plant quarterly to ensure even growth. Consistent light is key - they don't adapt well to changes in lighting conditions.

Water

Water thoroughly when top 1-2 inches of soil feels dry. Ensure pot has good drainage and empty saucer after watering. Water less in winter. Consistency is crucial - irregular watering can cause leaf drop. Use room temperature water and avoid wetting the leaves.

Temperature & Humidity

Prefers humidity around 40-60%. Use a humidifier or pebble tray if needed. Maintain temperatures between 65-75°F (18-24°C). Avoid cold drafts, air conditioning vents, and heating units - they're very sensitive to temperature fluctuations.

Soil & Fertilizer

Well-draining, rich potting mix. Add perlite for better drainage. Fertilize with balanced, water-soluble fertilizer monthly during growing season (spring and summer). Reduce or stop fertilizing in winter.

Benefits

  • Makes a dramatic architectural statement in any room
  • Excellent air purifier, removing indoor air pollutants
  • Can grow quite tall (6-10 feet indoors), creating a tree-like appearance
  • Large leaves provide a lush, tropical feel
  • Long-living plant that can thrive for many years with proper care
  • Can be pruned and shaped to maintain desired size and appearance

Common Problems

  • Brown spots on leaves - Usually indicates overwatering or bacterial infection
  • Leaf drop - Often caused by environmental stress, especially changes in temperature or light
  • Yellow leaves - Can indicate overwatering, underwatering, or nutrient deficiency
  • Red or brown spots - Typically sunburn from too much direct light
  • Drooping leaves - Usually means the plant needs water
  • Leggy growth - Insufficient light causing stretched growth between leaves

Seasonal Care

  • Clean leaves monthly with a damp cloth to remove dust and help with photosynthesis
  • Support larger plants with a stake if they begin to lean
  • Prune in spring or summer to control size and encourage branching
  • When repotting, only go up one pot size to avoid overwatering issues
  • Consider using a moisture meter to ensure consistent watering
  • Keep plant in the same spot once you find a suitable location

Pruning & Growth

To encourage branching and maintain shape:

  • Prune during growing season (spring/summer)
  • Make cuts just above a leaf node
  • Remove no more than 10% of the plant at once
  • Use clean, sharp pruning shears
  • Consider propagating pruned sections

Warning Signs

Fiddle Leaf Figs often show stress through their leaves. Watch for early warning signs: slight leaf curling, dark spots, or leaves losing their glossy appearance. Address these issues promptly as the plant can decline quickly if problems aren't resolved.
